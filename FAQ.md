# FAQ

> Common questions about the handbook, the journey, and how to get the most from it.

---

## About the handbook

**Q: Who is this for?**
Working software developers who know Python and want to become production AI Engineers. It assumes engineering maturity and skips beginner programming.

**Q: Do I need a math degree?**
No. Module 02 builds the exact linear algebra, calculus, and probability you need — from intuition first. You need comfort with high-school math and a willingness to think.

**Q: Do I need a GPU?**
Not for most of the material. Early modules run on a laptop. For deep learning and fine-tuning, use free/cloud GPU options (covered in Module 00). Heavy training is optional — understanding it is not.

**Q: How long will this take?**
About **52 weeks at ~10 focused hours/week** (~570 hours total). Going slower is fine; skipping the retention system is not.

**Q: Is this tied to one AI provider?**
No. Concepts are provider-agnostic. Examples use widely available tools and SDKs; when building apps, default to the most capable current models (see [RESOURCES.md](RESOURCES.md)).

## How to study

**Q: Can I skip modules I already know?**
Skim, then **take the module quiz cold**. If you pass from memory, skip. If not, do the module. Don't skip based on familiarity alone — familiarity ≠ recall.

**Q: What's the single biggest mistake learners make?**
The "tutorial trap": reading/watching along and *feeling* like you understand. If you can't reproduce it on a blank page, you haven't learned it. See [LEARNING_STRATEGY.md](LEARNING_STRATEGY.md).

**Q: Reading feels slow. Can I just read and move on?**
You can, but you'll forget ~80% within weeks. The active-recall + spaced-repetition system exists precisely to prevent that. Trust it.

**Q: How do I know I'm ready to move to the next module?**
Pass the quiz, complete the project, and clear the milestone audit where one exists. Don't accumulate debt.

## Content & structure

**Q: Why is the numbering zero-padded and fixed?**
So folders sort correctly and links never break. Published modules/lessons are **never renumbered** — new content appends. See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md).

**Q: Where do exercises, quizzes, and projects live?**
Each has a top-level folder mirroring the module numbers. See [REPOSITORY_STRUCTURE.md](REPOSITORY_STRUCTURE.md).

**Q: The images are placeholders. Why?**
Illustrations are described in text first so the content is complete and reviewable before art is produced. Real assets replace placeholders in `assets/images/`.

**Q: Can I contribute or adapt this?**
Yes — follow [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) so additions match the book's voice and structure.

## Career & interviews

**Q: When should I start interview prep?**
From Module 06 onward, one session per week in [interview-prep/](interview-prep/). Don't leave it to the end.

**Q: Will this get me a job?**
This builds the *capability* and a portfolio of real systems. Combined with the capstones and interview prep, it targets exactly what AI Engineering roles test: fundamentals, applied LLM skills, and production judgment.

**Q: What should my portfolio show?**
The Module 15 capstones: a deployed, observable, evaluated RAG service and a tool-using agent — with writeups explaining your design decisions.
